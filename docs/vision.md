# Vision

# Ashwood County

Ashwood County is a third-person 3D zombie survival game focused on exploration, tension and believable human stories.

The player is not a superhero.

The world should feel dangerous, lonely and unpredictable.

Combat is only one way to survive.

The game should encourage:

- exploration
- scavenging
- careful decision making
- resource management
- avoiding unnecessary fights
- helping or abandoning other survivors

---

# Core Pillars

## 1. Atmosphere

The world should feel abandoned but believable.

Quiet roads, empty houses, forests, weather, abandoned vehicles and environmental storytelling are more important than constant action.

---

## 2. Player Freedom

Players should be free to decide:

- where to explore
- who to trust
- when to fight
- when to flee
- what resources matter

---

## 3. Meaningful Survival

Resources should always matter.

Food.

Medicine.

Weapons.

Fuel.

Time.

Every decision should have consequences.

---

## 4. Believable NPCs

Long-term goal.

Survivors should behave like people rather than scripted quest givers.

Relationships should emerge naturally from gameplay.

---

## 5. Exploration

The world should reward curiosity.

Buildings should contain useful resources, environmental storytelling and occasional danger.

Players should frequently ask:

> "Should I risk going inside?"

---

## 6. Persistent World Resources

Items should exist inside the world rather than appearing directly in the player's possession.

Cars, cupboards, lockers, corpses and other containers maintain their own inventories.

Searching reveals what a container holds.

The player chooses:

- What to take
- What to leave
- What to return for later
- Whether limited inventory space is worth using

Uncollected items should remain where they were found.

This supports exploration, resource planning and the feeling that locations continue to exist independently of the player.
---

# Prototype Goal

The prototype is **not** the full game.

Its purpose is to answer:

- Is Godot suitable?
- Is the Compatibility renderer sufficient?
- Can AI-assisted development realistically build this game?
- Is the gameplay fun?

Every implemented feature should move toward answering those questions.

Avoid adding systems that do not improve the prototype.
